The Hanging Gardens of Babylon

Location: Babylon (near present-day Hillah, Iraq)

Historical Notes:
- Time Period: Believed to have been built in the 6th century BCE during the reign of King Nebuchadnezzar II, although its existence remains a topic of debate among historians.

- Significance: Described as a remarkable feat of engineering with an ascending series of tiered gardens.

- Construction: Said to have used an advanced irrigation system, possibly an Archimedes screw.

- Purpose: Possibly built to cheer up Nebuchadnezzar's homesick wife, Amytis of Media.



Best time to visit

- Optimal Time: October to April - Reason: More temperate weather and less intense heat than the summer months.

- Note: Always check current travel advisories due to potential instability in the region.
